    Data Groups

    Users can be divided into different groups, each having a different view of the data being stored. Each team or departments use of the data is going to be different, business users and support staff will have different ways of representing data stored in the system. Data groups control the views of the data and enforces rules that restricts or allows access to data.

    Different teams have different questions to ask of the data.

    Logs BAMOps TeamSupport
    Web Logs How Much traffic are we getting ? How Effective is our AD Campaigns ? How much of traffic is converting ?Latency, Page Load times, 404 errors, failed requests, bandwidth utilization etc 404 errors
    Payment Logs Revenue, Average basket size, average number of items in each basket, payment errors Payment Errors, Failed Payments,...
    Cloud MetricsHow much is the cloud costing us to run in the clouds ? Burst rate, How many nodes are we using, What is our capacity and utilization ? ...

    1.) Create the a Datagroup for each team

    For each datagroup add the data sources or tags to be made available to the users.

    The datagroups for support and the ops teams is set up in a similar fashion.

    2.) Custom Group Workspaces When a user logs in they will end up at the bam.Home page. Each Search and Workspace saved by a user will be namespaced to their particular datagroup. Workspaces can be linked together turning the bam.Home Workspace into a workflow that users of the business team can follow. The Home page provides a high level view of business activity and the navigation panel links to other Workspaces providing more detail. These workspaces can also be attached to emails as reports.

    3.) Different Views of the Same data Each team set's up workspaces which would be of interest to them. In this example there is some overlap between the data requirements of the Ops team and the BAM team, yet their homepages provide vastly different views of the data.

    The BAM workspace gives a very business centric view of the data, with a focus on costs and revenue.

    The ops workspace gives a view on the performance, capacity and health of the environment. Users of each team can take advantage of Workspace linking to create a workflow for team users to follow.

    4.) Create a Workflow As the team finds new ways of using the data a network of related Workspaces can be created using linking between workspaces.

    Using data groups users from all areas of your organisation can gain insight into operational and business intelligence metrics
